Amazon Consumer Sentiment and Behavior Analysis Based on Artificial Intelligence

Weiyi Chen

1 INTRODUCTION

Sentimental analysis is one of the most important parts in the artificial intelligence. It is a kind of technology in Natural Language Processing and a common method in text excavating. Its main function is to determine whether a given paragraph or sentence is positive or negative based on the known text and emotional symbols.

Sentimental analysis is an interdisciplinary subject of economics and sociology, which is an important part of market research. When large-scale information needed to be analyzed, the way analyzing by human eyes is extremely inefficient.
On amazon or twitter, people post their comments about items, events or news. These comments may be good or bad, and the sentimental analysis is used to categorize this information. As a result merchants can use sentimental analysis tools to know users’ experience and evaluation of their products directly instead of finding these feedbacks from many comments. A good handling of sentimental analysis can greatly improve the efficiency of people’s understanding of things, and can also use the conclusion of sentimental analysis to serve other people or things.

Furthermore, many financial companies use people’s views and attitudes on a certain company, a certain industry and a certain thing to predict their future trend of stocks to help them make trading decision.

Artificial intelligence is a branch of computer science. People are trying to make a kind of machine that can simulate human thought patterns in response. It is the process that simulate people’s cognition and analysis of information including the simulation of consciousness. Machine Learning is seen as a subset of artificial intelligence. Machine learning algorithms build a mathematical model to train data to make the decisions without program.

2 DATASET

The Dataset used contains reviews of unlocked mobile phones sold on Amazon.com. The data set can be used to find out insights with respect to reviews, ratings, price and their relationships. Data was acquired in December, 2016 by the crawlers. The dataset contains 124,152 data entries and 6 features: Product Name, Brand Name, Price, Rating Reviews and Review Votes.

4 Machine Learning Problem Formulation

Our problem is a binary classification problem. The input data is the text of customers’ phone review, the output is 0/1 binary labels which represents the customers’ preference for this phone – like or dislike.

5 Feature Engineering

The bags-of-words (BoW) is the model that help computer convert text into numerical vectors. This program ignores grammar and word order, and the text is only regarded as a collection of words, and the appearance of each word in the document is independent.

However, here are some problems of bags of words are that any information about the order or structure of words in the sentence is discarded.

A way to contain the information of words order is using ngrams, which denotes a contiguous sequence of n items from a given sample of text or speech. Thus, compared with a bag-of-words model, bag-of-n-grams, can describe better on the phrases and turning predicate such as ‘not like’, ‘not agree’, which is common in a review.

7 Experimental Design and Results

7.1 Experimental Design
The data set is divided into training, validation and test data set. Models are trained and tuned on training data set.

Hyper-parameters are selected using validation data set. Then, we use tuned models to predict labels on the test data set.

We used two methods of feature engineering. One is bag-of-words and another is bag-of-Ngrams. Experiment is set up for comparing those different feature engineering methods. The metric we are using is F1-Score.

7.2 Results and Analysis

Result Table

We use both bags of words and bags of Ngrams to collect data, compare to the method of Bags of Words and bags of Ngrams, we found out that the performance of the bags of Ngrams is higher than bags of words.

The reason causes the difference is the information loss of the Bags of Words, however, the Bags of Ngrams still keep some order of the information, as a result it has a better performance.

8 Conclusion

In this report, we use machine learning to detect sentiment information of custom reviews. We compare two vector representation / feature engineering methods of documents, which is bag-of-words and bag-of-ngram. Then, logistic regression is used as our classification model.

Our experiments on Amazon Phone Review dataset shows that benefiting from the ability to capture the order information of words, bag-of-ngrams has the best performance.
